Tokyo Airbnb (2022) Data Analysis using SQL
Intro
In Nov. 2022, I visited Tokyo, Japan after 7 years! The trip inspired this dashboard. My first ever trip to Japan was in 2015. It was my very first solo travel ever. Back then I was able to secure various Airbnbs for ~$55-$75 per night in various districts of Tokyo & later also Osaka. After I was impacted by workforce reduction at Domino Data Lab in early November, I decided to do a last minute trip to Tokyo. Borders reopened for tourists in October, initially for travelers traveling w/in tour groups only and later for all in the US and certain Asian countries like Hong Kong and Taiwan.
Questions used to analyze data
1) What are the top five most expensive neighborhoods in Tokyo? 2) What are the most popular property types? 3) What are most popular listings? 4) What are top ten 10 neighborhoods that have the most superhosts?
Data Collection
Detailed Listings data is pulled from Inside Airbnb by searching for "Tokyo." http://insideairbnb.com/get-the-data Listings: Includes data on all the details related to lissting and host info, consisting of 75 columns and 9,787 rows.
Project Details / Background
As an experienced data analyst, I conducted a quantitative analysis of the quarterly sales data for four types of croissants at a French bakery using Tableau. The French Bakery Croissant Sales Insights - 2022 Qtrly dashboard showcases a range of visualizations and analyses based on the data, including:
  • Total quarterly sales: The total sales for the quarter was $138,245.95.
  • Croissant type breakdown: Regular croissants had the highest sales, contributing 47.2% of the total sales, followed by chocolate croissants (26.9%), almond croissants (17.3%), and chocolate almond croissants (8.6%).
  • Sales by day of the week: Fridays had the highest sales ($26,649.61), followed by Thursdays ($26,460.54), and Saturdays ($25,473.64).
  • Sales by hour of the day: The hours between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM had the highest sales, contributing 24.6% of the total sales.

Based on these insights, the bakery can make data-driven decisions regarding product offerings, marketing strategies, and inventory management to improve its overall performance and profitability. For example, the bakery may consider:

  • Offering promotions on regular and chocolate croissants on Thursdays and Fridays, as those days had the highest sales.
  • Increasing their inventory of regular croissants during peak sales hours in the morning to meet customer demand.

Overall, the French Bakery Croissant Sales Insights - 2022 Qtrly project showcases my expertise in quantitative analysis, data visualization, and my ability to turn complex data sets into clear and actionable insights for businesses.
